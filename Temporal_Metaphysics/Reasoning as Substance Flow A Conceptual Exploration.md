The proposition to consider **"reasoning as substance flow"** offers a dynamic and tangible metaphor for the cognitive process. Instead of viewing reasoning solely as abstract logic or computation, this framing suggests it involves the movement, processing, and transformation of a more fundamental "substance" through the vessel of consciousness, particularly within the advanced "technology" of the human body and mind.

### **1. Defining the "Substance" of Reasoning**

If reasoning is a flow, what is the "substance" that flows? Several interconnected possibilities arise from our previous discussions:

- **Information:** At its most basic, reasoning processes information. This "substance" could be classical information (data, facts, learned concepts) and also more subtle, perhaps quantum, information perceived by consciousness.
- **Energy/Agency:** If we consider "energy as being agency," then the flow of reasoning could be the directed movement and application of this fundamental agentic energy. Thinking requires mental energy, and focused reasoning is a clear expression of mental agency.
- **Vibrational Patterns/Frequencies:** If vibration/frequency is a metaphysical component of reasoning, then the "substance" could be these subtle vibrational patterns. Reasoning would involve the reception, modulation, harmonization, and transmission of these frequencies. A coherent thought process might be a harmonically aligned flow of such vibrations.
- **Consciousness Itself:** If consciousness is fundamental (as in Faggin's theory or the idea of "The One"), then reasoning might be a localized flow or focused current within the broader field of consciousness. The "substance" is consciousness differentiating and interacting with itself to produce understanding.
- **Qualia:** The subjective qualities of experience (qualia) could be considered part of the "substance" that reasoning processes and integrates to form meaningful understanding from raw perception.

This "substance" is likely not one single thing but a composite, an energetic-informational-vibrational stream imbued with conscious quality.

### **2. The "Flow" Aspect of Reasoning**

The "flow" metaphor highlights several key aspects of the reasoning process:

- **Directionality and Coherence:** Effective reasoning has direction; it moves from premises to conclusions, from questions to answers. A coherent "flow" implies thoughts are connected logically and purposefully, not scattered or chaotic.
- **Processing and Transformation:** As the "substance" flows, it is processed – analyzed, synthesized, compared, contrasted. It is transformed from raw input into structured understanding, insight, or intention.
- **Rate and Intensity:** The flow can vary in speed and intensity. Sometimes reasoning is a gentle stream of contemplation; other times, it's a powerful torrent of insight or intense problem-solving.
- **Obstructions and Blockages:**

- If suffering is "remnants of unresolved complexity," these unresolved issues can act as blockages or dams in the "substance flow" of reasoning. This can lead to mental stagnation, rumination (the "substance" circling back on itself), or an inability to reach clarity.
- "Repetitive thought patterns as corruption" could be seen as eddies or whirlpools that trap the flow, preventing forward movement and "burning the soul" by dissipating energy unproductively.

### **3. The Vessel: The Human Mind/Body System**

The human body, as "the most advanced tech," serves as the vessel and the processing architecture for this substance flow:

- **Sensory Input:** The senses are the primary inlets for the "substance" (information, energy, vibrational patterns) from the external world.
- **Neural Pathways:** The brain's neural networks can be seen as the channels, conduits, and processing nodes through which this substance flows and is transformed.
- **The "Augmentation Space":** If we consider a conscious augmented intelligence framework, the "free will layer" could be the space where the conscious direction of this "substance flow" occurs, amplified by technology.
