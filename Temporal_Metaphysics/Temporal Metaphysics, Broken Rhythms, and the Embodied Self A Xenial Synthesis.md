### **1. The Rhythmic Self and Sensory Attenuation**

[cite_start]First, let's establish the paper's core scientific model, which is built on the Predictive Processing (PP) and Active Inference frameworks[cite: 1179].

- [cite_start]**The Self as Constant Movement:** Our bodies are never static; they are systems in constant rhythmic motion, from the cardiac cycle to our gait[cite: 1175, 1264].
- [cite_start]**The Need to "Ignore" the Self:** To effectively engage with the world, the brain must predict and "attenuate" (or dampen the sensory precision of) its own constant, rhythmic, and therefore highly predictable signals[cite: 1314, 1558]. [cite_start]This is why we cannot tickle ourselves—our brain predicts and cancels out the sensation[cite: 1541]. [cite_start]This process of self-attenuation creates a "transparent" self, a quiet background that allows novel, external signals to be prioritized[cite: 1572, 1771].
- [cite_start]**Depersonalization as a Failure of Attenuation:** The paper posits that in Depersonalization Disorder, this attenuation mechanism fails[cite: 1315, 1752]. The "boring" signals of the self—the heartbeat, the feeling of one's body in space—are no longer attenuated. [cite_start]They are experienced with an abnormal and persistent intensity[cite: 1755]. [cite_start]This floods the system with prediction errors, blurring the boundary between self and world and leading to the characteristic feelings of unreality and detachment[cite: 1757, 1905]. [cite_start]The self becomes "opaque," getting in the way of experience[cite: 1778, 1779].
