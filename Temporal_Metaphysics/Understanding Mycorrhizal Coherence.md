Foundations of Mycorrhizal Symbiosis

Mycorrhizae (from Greek "mycos" for fungus and "rhiza" for root) form mutualistic partnerships where fungi colonize plant roots, extending their reach into soil for nutrients (e.g., phosphorus, nitrogen) in exchange for plant-derived carbons. microbewiki.kenyon.edu

 Dominant types include arbuscular mycorrhizal (AM) fungi (e.g., Glomeromycota, associating with ~80% of plants) and ectomycorrhizal (EM) fungi (e.g., Basidiomycota, common in forests). frontiersin.org +1

Coherence emerges from this symbiosis: Fungi act as bioelectric conduits, enabling plants to "communicate" and share resources across networks spanning meters or hectares, fostering emergent harmony akin to a planetary nervous system.
