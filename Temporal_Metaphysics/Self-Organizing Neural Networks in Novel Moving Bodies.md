**Core Findings of the Neurobot Paper:**

1. **Neural Self-Organization in Novel Embodiments:** Neural precursor cells, when combined with embryonic skin cells (*Xenopus* ectoderm), can differentiate into functional neurons and self-organize into networks *within* a completely artificial, evolutionarily unprecedented "biobot" body.
2. **Emergent Functionality and Behavior:** These "neurobots" exhibit distinct morphology (more elongated) and significantly more complex spontaneous movement patterns compared to control biobots lacking neurons. This demonstrates that the self-organized neural network influences the collective behavior of the skin cells (motor effectors).
3. **Functional Neural Activity:** Calcium imaging confirms that the neurons within the neurobots are functionally active, displaying spontaneous signaling and suggesting connectivity.
4. **Transcriptional Reprogramming:** The presence of neurons dramatically alters the gene expression profile (transcriptome) of the entire construct. Neurobots show significant upregulation of genes related to:
   - Nervous system development, synapse formation, neuron projection.
   - Neurotransmitter receptors (glutamatergic, GABAergic, cholinergic, etc.).
   - *Visual perception* (opsins, retinal genes) – surprisingly, given the lack of eyes or light stimuli during development.
   - Extracellular matrix (ECM) components.
5. **Shift Towards Ancient Genes:** Phylostratigraphic analysis reveals that the neurobot transcriptome is significantly enriched for more ancient genes ("All living organisms," "Eukaryota") compared to controls, suggesting a reversion to more fundamental biological principles when placed in a novel context.
6. **Plasticity Beyond Evolutionary History:** The study demonstrates the remarkable plasticity of neural tissue, showing its ability to develop structure and function outside its typical developmental pathway and evolutionary constraints.
