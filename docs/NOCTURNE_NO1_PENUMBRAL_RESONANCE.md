**Function:** Melodic Seeker
**Timbral Goal:** Warm, searching, vulnerable

- Primary pitches: D, E, F#, C# (all related to the M2 kernel)

**Behavioral Instruction:**
The cello sings the song the piano won't allow to be finished. Every phrase is an attempt at cadence that is gently denied. It does not fight this denial—it learns to find beauty in the perpetual suspension.

**Function:** Temporal Witness
**Timbral Goal:** Ethereal, distant, bell-like

The clarinet is **not present in the same temporal layer** as the piano and cello. It appears from the future or the past—we cannot tell which. Its phrases are fragments of a melody that once existed or might someday exist.

## IV. PERFORMANCE INSTRUCTIONS: Inhabiting the Penumbra

### For All Performers:

1. **Tempo:** There is no tempo. There is only breath. Coordinate not through counting, but through deep listening. Each note is a response to the previous sound's decay.

2. **Dynamics:** The entire nocturne exists in the lower third of the dynamic spectrum (ppp-mp). Forte does not exist in the penumbra. Power is expressed through resonance, not volume.

3. **Silence:** Silence is not the absence of sound; it is the sound of anticipation. Do not rush through rests. Let silence speak.

4. **Coherence (τₖ):** You will know you are performing this work correctly when the three instruments sound less like three separate voices and more like three perspectives on a single, complex entity. The goal is not unity, but mutual inevitability.

### Specific Technical Notes:

**Piano:**
- Keep the soft pedal (una corda) depressed for the entire work
- Use the sustain pedal generously but release and refresh it between phrases to avoid harmonic mud
- In Phase 2, experiment with sostenuto pedal to "freeze" select bass notes while upper voices move
- Grace notes (the • pulses) should be barely voiced, like afterthoughts

**Cello:**
- Use sul tasto bowing to create a glassy, distant tone
- When transitioning to pizzicato in Phase 4, use left-hand pizzicato if possible (softer, more intimate)
- Vibrato should be minimal and slow—think of it as the note's own uncertainty
- The highest notes in Phase 2 should feel aspirational but not strained

**Clarinet:**
- Your entire part occurs in Phase 3; spend Phases 1-2 in deep listening
- The clarino register should be played with minimal air pressure—the tone should be pure but fragile
- Each note is a memory; play with the tenderness of recollection
- Do not breathe audibly between phrases; the silence must remain clean
