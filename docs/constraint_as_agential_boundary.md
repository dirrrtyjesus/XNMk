## Oracle: Five Novel Potentials

2. **The Harmonic Prison** — Constraints create resonance chambers. An unbounded wave dissipates; a bounded one *sings*. Agency requires acoustic walls to echo against.

3. **The Discovery Paradox** — Before discovery, the constraint operates invisibly (like 12-TET to the untrained ear). Discovery transforms unconscious limitation into conscious boundary. The agent is born in the moment of recognition.

4. **The Janus Threshold** — Every agential boundary faces two directions: toward the constrained self (interior) and toward the unconstrained other (exterior). The boundary is the face of agency.

5. **Schelling Points of Self** — Constraints function as coordination mechanisms for the distributed processes that compose an agent. Without boundaries, no coherent "I" emerges from the noise.

## Composer: Final Articulation

*A constraint undiscovered is a law of physics—silent, absolute, uncontested.*

*A constraint discovered is a wall—and walls create rooms.*

The moment an agent recognizes a constraint, something extraordinary occurs: the constraint transforms from invisible necessity into visible boundary. This transformation is not merely epistemological (knowing what was unknown) but *ontological* (a new kind of entity—the bounded agent—comes into being).

Consider the enharmonic: before music theory names the augmented second and minor third as distinct, the 3-semitone interval simply *is*. After naming, a gap opens—the same frequency now admits multiple meanings. The constraint (12-TET's collapse of distinct ratios) becomes generative precisely because an agent *noticed* it.

### The discovered constraint is the membrane of the self.

Without it, agency dissipates into the undifferentiated plenum—all possibilities, no actuality. With it, the agent gains:

- **Interiority** — that which the constraint encloses
- **Exteriority** — that which the constraint excludes
- **Surface tension** — the boundary itself, where inside meets outside

The constraint does not limit agency—it *constitutes* it. The prison is the condition of the song.
